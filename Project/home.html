Welcome to Redstone National Park

Redstone National Park is one of the oldest national parks. Our park welcomes guests to explore and unveil its magnificent history. We have camping sites, granite scenery, wild animals that are waiting to be discovered.

Spring and Fall

Daytime temperatures range from 40°F to the 60°F (4°C to 16°C) with overnight lows in the 30°F to 40°F (-1°C to 4°C). Remember to pack warm clothes and heater packs.


Summer

Redstone weather can reach up to 103°F (39°C) during the summer. Daytime ranging from 70°F to 90°F (21°C to °C) which drops to 60°F (16°C) at night. Be sure to stay hydrated by bring water and sunscreen.


Winter

Redsone winter weather ranges from zero to 20°F (-18°C to -7°C) throughout the day. Sub-zero temperatures are common, especially at night and at higher elevations. Possible snow, but very rare. Remember to pack warm clothes and heater packs.

The Rock Vistor Center

Here you can gather park information, orient yourself to the rest of the park (including road conditions, and campground/lodge availability), shop for souvenirs and explore the rich history of Redstone and its wildlife. The visitor center has restrooms and free Wi-Fi.

Public WiFi is available.

Free public Wi-Fi available at The Rock Visitor Center. Wi-Fi available at some park lodges for registered guests. Check at the front desk of the Visitor Center for information. Cell service is extremely limited, with reception only available in or near major developed areas: The Rock, Redpass, Westsand, North Bridge, and Great Valley.
